Cayley’s Theorem🔗

Cayley’s theorem says that any group GGG admits a representation as a subgroup of a symmetric group, specifically the symmetric group acting on the underlying set of GGG .

To start with, we note that any element xxx of GGG determines a bijection on the underlying set of GGG , by multiplication with xxx on the left. The inverse of this bijection is given by multiplication with x−1x^{-1}x−1 , and the proof that these are in fact inverse functions are given by the group laws:

Cayley : G .fst → G .fst ≃ G .fst
Cayley x = Iso→Equiv bij where
  bij : Iso _ _
  bij .fst y = x ⋆ y
  bij .snd .isIso.inv y = inverse x ⋆ y
  bij .snd .isIso.rinv y =
    x ⋆ (inverse x ⋆ y) ≡⟨ associative ⟩≡
    (x ⋆ inverse x) ⋆ y ≡⟨ ap₂ _⋆_ inverseʳ refl ⟩≡
    unit ⋆ y            ≡⟨ idˡ ⟩≡
    y                   ∎
  bij .snd .isIso.linv y =
    inverse x ⋆ (x ⋆ y) ≡⟨ associative ⟩≡
    (inverse x ⋆ x) ⋆ y ≡⟨ ap₂ _⋆_ inverseˡ refl ⟩≡
    unit ⋆ y            ≡⟨ idˡ ⟩≡
    y                   ∎

We then show that this map is a group homomorphism from GGG to Sym(G)\mathrm{Sym}(G)Sym(G) :

Cayley-isHom : isGroupHom G (Sym G-set) Cayley
Cayley-isHom .isGroupHom.pres-⋆ x y = Σ≡Prop isProp-isEquiv (funext lemma) where
  lemma : (e : G .fst) → (x ⋆ y) ⋆ e ≡ x ⋆ (y ⋆ e)
  lemma e = sym associative

Finally, we show that this map is injective; Thus, GGG embeds as a subgroup of Sym(G)\mathrm{Sym}(G)Sym(G) (the image of Cayley).

Cayley-injective : injective Cayley
Cayley-injective {x} {y} eqvs-equal =
  x                   ≡⟨ sym idʳ ⟩≡
  x ⋆ unit            ≡⟨⟩ 
  Cayley x .fst unit  ≡⟨ happly (ap fst eqvs-equal) unit ⟩≡
  Cayley y .fst unit  ≡⟨⟩
  y ⋆ unit            ≡⟨ idʳ ⟩≡
  y                   ∎
