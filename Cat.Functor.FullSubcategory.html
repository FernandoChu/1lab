A full subcategory D\ca{D}D of some larger category C\ca{C}C is the category generated by some predicate PPP on the objects of of C\ca{C}C: You keep only those objects for which PPP holds, and all the morphisms between them. An example is the category of abelian groups, as a full subcategory of groups: being abelian is a proposition (there‚Äôs ‚Äúat most one way for a group to be abelian‚Äù).
