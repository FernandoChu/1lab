Equivalencesüîó

A functor F:C‚ÜíDF : \ca{C} \to \ca{D}F:C‚ÜíD is an equivalence of categories when it has a right adjoint G:D‚ÜíDG : \ca{D} \to \ca{D}G:D‚ÜíD, with the unit and counit natural transformations being natural isomorphisms. This immediately implies that our adjoint pair F‚ä£GF \dashv GF‚ä£G extends to an adjoint triple F‚ä£G‚ä£FF \dashv G \dashv FF‚ä£G‚ä£F.

We chose, for definiteness, the above definition of equivalence of categories, since it provides convenient access to the most useful data: The induced natural isomorphisms, the adjunction unit/counit, and the triangle identities. It is a lot of data to come up with by hand, though, so we provide some alternatives:

Fully faithful, essentially surjectiveüîó
