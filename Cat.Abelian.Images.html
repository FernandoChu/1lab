Images in abelian categories🔗

Let f:A→Bf : A \to Bf:A→B be a morphism in an abelian category A\ca{A}A, which (by definition) admits a canonical decomposition as

A↠pcoker⁡(ker⁡f)≅ker⁡(coker⁡f)↪iB, A \xepi{p} \coker (\ker f) \cong \ker (\coker f) \xmono{i} B\text{,} Apcoker(kerf)≅ker(cokerf)i​B,

where the map ppp is epic, iii is monic, and the indicated isomorphism arises from fff in a canonical way, using the universal properties of kernels and cokernels. What we show in this section is that the arrow ker⁡(coker⁡f)↪B\ker (\coker f) \mono Bker(cokerf)↪B is an image for fff: It is the largest monomorphism through which fff factors. Since this construction does not depend on any specificities of fff, we conclude that every map in an abelian category factors as a regular epi followed by a regular mono.
