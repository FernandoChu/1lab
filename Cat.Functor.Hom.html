The Coyoneda Lemmaüîó

The Coyoneda lemma is, like it‚Äôs dual, a statement about presheaves. It states that ‚Äúevery presheaf is a colimit of representables‚Äù, which, in less abstract terms, means that every presheaf arises as some way of gluing together a bunch of (things isomorphic to) hom functors!

module _ (P : Functor (C ^op) (Sets h)) where

    module P = Functor P

  open El C P
  open Element
  open ElementHom

We start by fixing some presheaf PPP, and constructing a Cocone whose coapex is PPP. This involves a clever choice of diagram category: specifically, the [category of elements] of PPP. This may seem like a somewhat odd choice, but recall that the data contained in ‚à´P\int P‚à´P is the same data as PPP, just melted into a soup of points. The cocone we construct will then glue all those points back together into PPP.

This is done by projecting out of ‚à´P\int P‚à´P into C\ca{C}C via the [canonical projection], and then embedding C\ca{C}C into the category of presheaves over C\ca{C}C via the yoneda embedding. Concretely, what this diagram gives us is a bunch of copies of the hom functor, one for each px:P(X)px : P(X)px:P(X). Then, to construct the injection map, we can just use the (contravariant) functorial action of PPP to take a px:P(X)px : P(X)px:P(X) and a f:Hom(A,X)f : Hom(A, X)f:Hom(A,X) to a P(A)P(A)P(A). This map is natural by functoriality of PPP.
