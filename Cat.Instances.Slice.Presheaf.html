Slices of presheaf categories🔗

We prove that slices of a presheaf category are again presheaf categories. Specifically, for PPP a presheaf, we have an isomorphism PSh(C)/P≅PSh(∫P)\psh(\ca{C})/P \cong \psh(\int P)PSh(C)/P≅PSh(∫P), where ∫\int∫ denotes the category of elements of PPP.

An object in the slice PSh(C)/P\psh(\ca{C})/PPSh(C)/P consists of a functor QQQ together with a natural transformation η:P→Q\eta : P \to Qη:P→Q. To transform this data into a functor ∫P→Sets\int P \to \sets∫P→Sets, observe that for each element (x,s)(x, s)(x,s) in ∫P\int P∫P, the fibre ηx∗(s)\eta_x^*(s)ηx∗​(s) is a set. But why this choice in particular? Well, observe that ∫P\int P∫P is essentially the total space of PPP — so that what we’re doing here is proving an equivalence between fibrations and dependent functions! This is in line with the existence of object classifiers, and in the 1-categorical level, with slices of Sets.

In fact, since we have Sets=PSh(∗)\sets = \psh(*)Sets=PSh(∗), that latter equivalence is a special case of the one constructed here — where in the calculation below, ccc denotes the constant presheaf ∗↦S* \mapsto S∗↦S. The category of elements of a presheaf ∗↦S* \mapsto S∗↦S consists of pairs (x,e)(x, e)(x,e) where x:∗x : *x:∗, of which there is only one choice, and e:Se : Se:S.

module _ {P : Functor (C ^op) (Sets κ)} where
  private module P = Functor P

  slice-ob→presheaf
    : Ob (Slice Cat[ C ^op , Sets κ ] P)
    → Functor (∫ C P ^op) (Sets κ)
  slice-ob→presheaf sl .F₀ (elem x s) =
      fibre (sl .map .η x) s
    , Σ-is-hlevel 2 (sl .domain .F₀ _ .is-tr) λ _ → is-prop→is-set (P.₀ _ .is-tr _ _)

  slice-ob→presheaf sl .F₁ eh (i , p) =
      sl .domain .F₁ (eh .hom) i
    , happly (sl .map .is-natural _ _ _) _ ·· ap (P.₁ _) p ·· eh .commute

Keeping with the theme, in the other direction, we take a total space rather than a family of fibres, with fibration being the first projection fst:

  presheaf→slice-ob : Functor (∫ C P ^op) (Sets κ) → Ob (Slice Cat[ C ^op , Sets κ ] P)
  presheaf→slice-ob y = obj where
    obj : /-Obj {C = Cat[ _ , _ ]} P
    obj .domain .F₀ c =
        Σ[ sect ∈ ∣ P.₀ c ∣ ] ∣ y .F₀ (elem c sect) ∣
      , Σ-is-hlevel 2 (P.₀ _ .is-tr) λ _ → y .F₀ _ .is-tr
    obj .domain .F₁ f (x , p) = P.₁ f x , y .F₁ (elem-hom f refl) p
    obj .map .η x = fst

Since the rest of the construction is routine calculation, we present it without comment.

  slice→total : Functor (Slice Cat[ C ^op , Sets κ ] P) Cat[ (∫ C P) ^op , Sets _ ]
  slice→total = func where
    func : Functor _ Cat[ (∫ C P) ^op , Sets _ ]
    func .F₀ = slice-ob→presheaf
    func .F₁ {x} {y} h .η i (z , p) =
      h .map .η (i .ob) z , ap (λ e → e .η _ z) (h .commutes) ∙ p
    func .F₁ {x} {y} h .is-natural _ _ _ = funext λ i →
      Σ-prop-path (λ _ → P.₀ _ .is-tr _ _) (happly (h .map .is-natural _ _ _) _)

    func .F-id    = Nat-path (λ x → funext λ y → Σ-prop-path (λ _ → P.₀ _ .is-tr _ _) refl)
    func .F-∘ f g = Nat-path (λ x → funext λ y → Σ-prop-path (λ _ → P.₀ _ .is-tr _ _) refl)

  slice→total-is-ff : is-fully-faithful slice→total
  slice→total-is-ff {x} {y} = is-iso→is-equiv (iso inv rinv linv) where
    inv : Hom Cat[ ∫ C P ^op , Sets _ ] _ _
        → Slice Cat[ C ^op , Sets _ ] P .Hom _ _
    inv nt .map .η i o = nt .η (elem _ (x .map .η i o)) (o , refl) .fst

    inv nt .map .is-natural _ _ f = funext λ z →
        ap (λ e → nt .η _ e .fst) (Σ-prop-path (λ _ → P.₀ _ .is-tr _ _) refl)
      ∙ ap fst (happly (nt .is-natural _ _
          (elem-hom f (happly (sym (x .map .is-natural _ _ _)) _))) _)

    inv nt .commutes = Nat-path λ z → funext λ w →
      nt .η (elem _ (x .map .η _ _)) (w , refl) .snd

    rinv : is-right-inverse inv (F₁ slice→total)
    rinv nt = Nat-path λ o → funext λ where
      (z , p) → Σ-prop-path (λ _ → P.₀ _ .is-tr _ _)
        (λ i → nt .η (elem (o .ob) (p i)) (z , (λ j → p (i ∧ j))) .fst)

    linv : is-left-inverse inv (F₁ slice→total)
    linv sh = /-Hom-path (Nat-path (λ z → refl))

  open is-precat-iso
  slice→total-is-iso : is-precat-iso slice→total
  slice→total-is-iso .has-is-ff = slice→total-is-ff
  slice→total-is-iso .has-is-iso = is-iso→is-equiv isom where
    open is-iso
    isom : is-iso slice-ob→presheaf
    isom .inv = presheaf→slice-ob
